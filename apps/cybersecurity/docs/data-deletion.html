DCI Cybersecurity Labs — Delete Your Data

Last updated: April 2026

What data does DCI Cybersecurity Labs store?

DCI Cybersecurity Labs stores your display name, a computed student identifier (SHA-256 hash of your access code and display name), and lab completion timestamps in Google Cloud Firestore. Your browser also stores your session (access code, display name, student ID) in localStorage.

How to delete your local data

You can delete your locally stored session data directly from the app:

1 Open DCI Cybersecurity Labs
2 Tap the Settings tab (bottom navigation)
3 Tap Reset Progress
4 Confirm by tapping Reset Everything

This clears all session data from your browser's localStorage. You can also clear localStorage manually via your browser's developer tools.

Firestore data

Progress data stored in Firestore (completed lab records and timestamps) can be removed by your instructor. Contact your instructor or email us to request deletion.

What gets deleted

Local deletion removes your stored session (access code, display name, student ID) from this browser. Firestore deletion removes your student document and all associated lab completion records.
